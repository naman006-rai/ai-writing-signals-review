# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to keep the review conservative and quality-focused.

## Core Rule

Never score text as definitely AI-written, and never output a probability that AI was used. Score only how much human editorial review the text warrants. Keep three buckets separate: editorial quality problems, possible AI-like signals, and factual/source-grounding problems.

## Review Focus

Low:

- No strong signals.
- Only isolated weak indicators.
- Signals are plausibly explained by genre, house style, translation, or ordinary editing.
- Text contains context-specific detail and traceable claims.

Medium:

- Repeated generic language or formulaic structure.
- More than one signal category appears.
- Several claims need source checks.
- There are quality problems, but no direct chatbot or generated-markup residue.

High:

- Multiple independent strong signals appear.
- Text includes direct chatbot residue, placeholder text, malformed generated citations, or broken markup.
- Broad claims appear with weak or unverifiable sourcing.
- Style shifts suggest pasted-in material that needs review.

## Confidence

Low:

- Short sample.
- Weak indicators only.
- Strong false-positive explanations exist.

Medium:

- Enough text to see repeated patterns.
- Several signals point in the same direction.
- Some human explanations remain plausible.

High:

- Objective artifacts are present, such as invalid citation markers or chatbot disclaimers.
- Multiple categories reinforce the same concern.
- The review still must say: "This is not proof of AI authorship."

## Severity

Low:

- Style issue, common phrase, or isolated formatting choice.

Medium:

- Repeated formulaic wording, vague attribution, generic claims, or unsupported analysis.

High:

- Broken citation artifacts, fake references, chatbot disclaimers, placeholder text, or misleading source claims.

## False-Positive Handling

For every signal, include a false-positive warning. Good warnings mention plausible human causes such as house style, translation, marketing source material, template use, novice editing, accessibility formatting, or genre conventions.

## Revision Priority

1. Fix factual errors, unsupported claims, and broken citations.
2. Remove or source generic importance and promotional claims.
3. Replace vague attribution with named, verifiable sources.
4. Repair formatting or markup for the target venue.
5. Simplify formulaic language only when it improves clarity.
